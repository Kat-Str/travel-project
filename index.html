Why Lublin?

Exploring the Old Town and Beyond

Lublin, a hidden gem in the heart of Eastern Poland, has everything to offer for a perfect city break. The Old Town is undoubtedly the highlight of Lublin, where the cobbled streets, winding lanes, and colorful townhouses with intricate details make it an Instagram-worthy destination. Walking through one of the gates leading to the Old Town is like traveling back in time, and getting lost in the charming streets is an experience to remember.

The vibrant atmosphere of the Old Town is truly contagious, and it's easy to spend hours in one of the many cafes and pubs. But Lublin has much more to offer beyond the Old Town, and the central part of the city is equally picturesque. Lublin is a perfect example of a Central European city, with its distinctive architecture and unique cultural blend. The city's rich history and lively present create an unforgettable experience for travelers seeking to discover the hidden gems of Europe.

My favorite tourist attractions in Lublin

Lublin Castle

The National Museum in Lublin is one of the oldest and largest museums in Eastern Poland. It was created in 1914, and received its own building in 1923.

Address:

Zamkowa 9

What I like about it

The castle in Lublin is a fascinating piece of history that has been standing for over 700 years. Its rich history and stunning architecture make it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in culture and history.

Village Open Air Museum

Open-air museum depicting historical village life on lush grounds with plants & farm animals.

Address:

al. Warszawska 96

What I like about it

The Lublin Village Open Air Museum offers a unique opportunity to experience the traditional way of life in rural Poland. Visitors can explore the reconstructed village and learn about the cultural heritage and customs of the Lublin region.

Old Town

The Old Town of Lublin is a historic district that dates back to the 13th century, located in the heart of the city. Unique blend of Renaissance, Baroque, and Gothic architecture.
